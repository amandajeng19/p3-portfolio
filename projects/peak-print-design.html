Valentine’s Day Feature

Overview

As an Assistant Production Editor for SFU’s student newspaper The Peak, I designed print layouts for 4–5 pages each Production Day using Adobe InDesign. Implementing design feedback from the Production & Design Editor, all spreads were completed quickly and with a high level of quality.

Team

Production Team:​​​​​​​ Josh Ralla (Production & Design Editor), Amanda Eng and Jo Zhou (Assistant Production Editors)

Tools

Adobe InDesign, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Fresco

Sample Production Day Process

First Draft

I first skimmed each article and noted any layout suggestions to better understand this piece of writing. For this Valentine’s-themed article, I was given a stock photo to use, and was requested to visually separate the paragraphs about Greek and Hindu expressions of love.

This pastel colour palette and typeface pairing (Giulia and Questa Sans) were selected to resemble candy hearts. When sharing this first draft PDF with the editors, I commented that the chemistry pun stock photo didn't match the piece; they agreed and said they would find a replacement photo.

Second Draft

In this second iteration, I adjusted my approach: candy hearts are a Westernized interpretation of love and Valentine’s Day, yet the article’s purpose was to highlight diverse celebrations of love worldwide.

For this new approach, I added dashed lines to suggest maps, travelling, and exploration.

Finalized Layout & Custom Illustration

To replace the stock photo, I selected a few love-related ideas from the article and illustrated them using Adobe Illustrator, formatting them to look like Polaroid photos. These reinforced the travel theme, as if the writer had been exploring the world for snapshots of love in different cultures.

Lastly, I polished off the layout by adjusting the negative space between the columns and reducing the visual weight of the few separated paragraphs. I’m pleased with how this article and print design developed over the course of the day. I also appreciate the representation of so many cultures, reflecting the diversity of the SFU students reading the article.
